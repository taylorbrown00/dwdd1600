Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship refers to the responsible, ethical, and effective use of technology and the internet. It's about understanding how to engage safely, respectfully, and productively in the digital world

What is Digital Law?

Digital law is the set of rules and laws that govern how people use technology, the internet, and digital content.

What is AUP?

AUP stands for and Acceptable Use Policy, it is a set of rules that explain how technology, devices, and networks may or may not be used within an organization like a school, workplace, or website.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is using someone else's work, ideas, or words as your own without giving proper credit.

It doesn't matter whether it's done on purpose or on accident.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any action that gives a student an unfair advantage or misrepresents their learning.

Basically it is cheating or being dishonest in school work.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

AI-generated content (text, images, code, audio, video) may be used only when allowed and only in responsible ways. AI is a tool to support learning-not a replacement for thinking or original work.
