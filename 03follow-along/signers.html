Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, known for his large and bold signature.
Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration and later the third President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A leading statesman, inventor, and diplomat; the oldest signer at 70 years old.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence and later the second President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A key organizer of early revolutionary activities, including the Boston Tea Party.
Roger Sherman
Helped draft both the Declaration and later the U.S. Constitution.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest men in the colonies.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution calling for independence from Britain.
